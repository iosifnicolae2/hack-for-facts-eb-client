## Introduction

> **Key Insight**: Internal audit is your ally, not your adversary. Auditors help you identify problems before external bodies do—and before they become public scandals or personal liability.

Internal audit exists to help you succeed. Unlike external audit (Curtea de Conturi), which focuses on accountability and penalties, internal audit focuses on improvement and risk management. Understanding this distinction will transform how you work with auditors.

### Response Best Practices

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Accept valid findings professionally | Get defensive or blame others |
| Provide specific, measurable actions | Make vague promises |
| Set realistic deadlines | Over-promise and under-deliver |
| Name responsible individuals | Assign to "the department" |
| Explain disagreements with evidence | Argue without documentation |
| Focus on system fixes | Focus on individual mistakes |

### Implementation Status Categories

| Status | Definition | Required Action |
|--------|------------|-----------------|
| **Open** | Accepted but not started | Assign resources, begin work |
| **In Progress** | Work underway | Monitor progress, remove barriers |
| **Completed** | Implemented and verified | Document evidence, close |
| **Overdue** | Deadline passed | Escalate, request extension, prioritize |
| **Suspended** | On hold (with approval) | Document reason, set review date |

### Finding Classification

| Classification | Definition | Expected Response Time |
|----------------|------------|------------------------|
| **Critical** | Immediate threat to entity or significant legal exposure | 30 days |
| **High** | Significant control weakness, material error risk | 60 days |
| **Medium** | Moderate risk, control improvement needed | 90 days |
| **Low** | Minor issue, best practice recommendation | 180 days |
| **Observation** | Opportunity for improvement, no risk | Optional |

**B) Internal control is ongoing operations; internal audit is periodic assessment**

Internal control refers to the policies, procedures, and activities that happen continuously as part of operations (like CFPP, segregation of duties, reconciliations). Internal audit is a separate function that periodically assesses whether internal controls are working effectively.
</details>

### Question 2
You receive a draft audit report with a finding you believe is factually incorrect. What is the proper response?

- A) Ignore it; auditors are always right
- B) Respond within 15 working days with documentary evidence
- C) Wait for the final report, then appeal to Curtea de Conturi
- D) Request the auditor be removed for incompetence

<details>
<summary>Show Answer</summary>

**B) Respond within 15 working days with documentary evidence**

The draft report phase exists precisely for this purpose—to allow auditees to present evidence and corrections before the report is finalized. You must respond in writing within 15 working days with specific documentation supporting your position. Silence is treated as acceptance.
</details>

### Question 3
Which statement about internal auditor independence is correct?

- A) Internal auditors report through the CFO to ensure financial expertise
- B) Internal auditors can be reassigned to operations if understaffed
- C) Internal auditors report directly to the ordonator principal
- D) Internal auditors can audit areas where they previously worked

<details>
<summary>Show Answer</summary>

**C) Internal auditors report directly to the ordonator principal**

Per Legea 672/2002, internal audit must report directly to the entity's management (ordonator principal), not through operational managers like the CFO. This ensures independence. Auditors cannot be assigned operational duties and cannot audit activities they participated in within the past 2 years.
</details>

### Question 4
Your institution received 10 audit findings last year. 8 were addressed, 2 remain overdue. What is the likely consequence?

- A) The overdue findings are automatically closed after 1 year
- B) Curtea de Conturi will impose immediate sanctions
- C) Next year's audit will prioritize these areas and may escalate
- D) The internal audit function is abolished for failure

<details>
<summary>Show Answer</summary>

**C) Next year's audit will prioritize these areas and may escalate**

Overdue findings don't disappear—they indicate persistent control weaknesses. Internal audit will prioritize follow-up, and the findings may be escalated in severity. If the pattern continues, it may be reported to UCAAPI or noted in the annual audit report, potentially triggering external audit attention.
</details>

## Key Takeaways

1. **Internal audit is your partner** - View auditors as allies who help you find and fix problems before external bodies do

2. **The draft report is your opportunity** - Respond within 15 days with evidence; silence means acceptance

3. **Findings require action plans** - Specific, measurable, realistic actions with named responsible persons

4. **Track implementation rigorously** - Overdue findings don't disappear; they accumulate and escalate

5. **Protect auditor independence** - Support their access, budget, and autonomy; obstruction is a criminal offense
