## Introduction

> **Key Insight**: Crises test your systems under pressure. The rules still apply—but knowing which flexibilities exist and how to use them properly protects both your institution and your personal liability.

Budget crises can arise from natural disasters, health emergencies, economic shocks, or internal management failures. Each requires different responses, but all demand that you maintain legal compliance while acting quickly.

### Escalation Authorities

| Level | Authority | Actions Available | Documentation |
|-------|-----------|-------------------|---------------|
| **Level 1** | Ordonator | Virări, reserve fund, payment prioritization | Internal memo, CFPP register |
| **Level 2** | Council/Board | Rectificare, borrowing, cuts | HCL/Decision, public notice |
| **Level 3** | External | State emergency funds, prefect supervision | Formal requests, declarations |

## Knowledge Check

Test your understanding of crisis management.

### Question 1
A flood damages a public building. Can you use the reserve fund for repairs?

- A) No, reserve fund is only for budget deficits
- B) Yes, but only with state authorization
- C) Yes, with HCL/decision approval for emergency/unforeseen needs
- D) Yes, without any approval in emergencies

<details>
<summary>Show Answer</summary>

**C) Yes, with HCL/decision approval for emergency/unforeseen needs**

Per Legea 273/2006, Art. 36, the reserve fund can be used for unforeseen and urgent needs, including natural disaster response. However, usage requires approval from the local council (HCL) or equivalent governing body. The ordonator cannot unilaterally use the reserve fund.
</details>

### Question 2
Emergency procurement (negotiation without publication) can be used when:

- A) The budget didn't plan for this purchase
- B) Extreme urgency from unforeseen events makes normal procedures impossible
- C) The preferred supplier only responds to direct requests
- D) It's year-end and we need to spend the budget

<details>
<summary>Show Answer</summary>

**B) Extreme urgency from unforeseen events makes normal procedures impossible**

Art. 104(1)(c) of Legea 98/2016 allows negotiation without publication only for extreme urgency caused by events unforeseeable by the contracting authority, where time limits for other procedures cannot be met. Poor planning, convenience, or budget pressure are NOT valid justifications.
</details>

### Question 3
You have cash for either salaries OR a critical supplier payment, but not both. What do you do?

- A) Split available cash between both
- B) Pay salaries first; negotiate with supplier
- C) Pay supplier first to avoid contract breach
- D) Pay neither until more cash arrives

<details>
<summary>Show Answer</summary>

**B) Pay salaries first; negotiate with supplier**

Salaries are legally mandated payments (labor law, social contributions). Delaying them creates personal liability and potential labor disputes. Supplier payments, while important, can often be negotiated for brief delays. Document everything and communicate proactively with the supplier.
</details>

### Question 4
What percentage of the expenditure budget can be held in the reserve fund for local authorities?

- A) Up to 10%
- B) Exactly 5%
- C) Up to 5%
- D) As much as needed

<details>
<summary>Show Answer</summary>

**C) Up to 5%**

Per Legea 273/2006, Art. 36, the reserve fund for local budgets can be up to 5% of the total expenditure budget. It's a maximum, not a requirement, but having a reserve fund is standard practice for managing unforeseen events.
</details>

## Key Takeaways

1. **Crises don't suspend the rules** - Flexibilities exist, but documentation and legal basis are still required

2. **Reserve fund is first defense** - Up to 5%, HCL approval, emergency purposes only

3. **Emergency procurement is high-risk** - Use only when genuinely necessary; document extensively

4. **Payment priority protects you** - Salaries first, then time-sensitive, then contractual

5. **Documentation is your protection** - If audited later, your records defend your decisions
