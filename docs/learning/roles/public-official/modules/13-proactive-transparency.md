## Introduction

> **Key Insight**: Transparency is not about "being open when convenient"—it's a legal obligation with personal consequences. Understanding what you MUST publish versus what you CAN withhold protects both citizens' rights AND your professional position.

Romania's Freedom of Information Act (Legea 544/2001) creates two types of transparency obligations:

1. **Proactive**: Information you must publish without anyone asking
2. **Reactive**: Information you must provide when requested

| **Legal Framework** | Laws, regulations | Website | On change |

| **Decisions** | Council/board decisions | Website, gazette | 5 days of adoption |
| **Procurement** | Notices, awards | SEAP, website | Per procedure |

### Common Exemption Mistakes

| Mistake | Why It's Wrong | Correct Approach |
|---------|----------------|------------------|
| "It's internal" | Internal ≠ exempt | Disclose unless specific exemption applies |
| "It might embarrass us" | Embarrassment ≠ exemption | Must have legal basis for withholding |
| "Requester might misuse it" | Cannot assess intent | Provide to all equally |
| "Too much work to compile" | Administrative burden ≠ exemption | Request extension if needed |
| "Commercial secret" (blanket) | Must do public interest test | Balance secrecy vs. public interest |

## Knowledge Check

Test your understanding of transparency obligations.

### Question 1
A journalist requests copies of all emails between your department and a contractor regarding a recent project. Which response is correct?

- A) Refuse entirely—internal communications are exempt
- B) Provide all emails as requested
- C) Provide emails, redacting personal data but not business content
- D) Claim commercial secret for the entire correspondence

<details>
<summary>Show Answer</summary>

**C) Provide emails, redacting personal data but not business content**

Internal communications are NOT automatically exempt. You should provide the correspondence, redacting only truly protected information (personal data like CNP, personal email addresses of individuals). Business discussions about a public contract are public interest information and should be disclosed.
</details>

### Question 2
A FOIA request arrives on Monday, November 4. When is the response deadline?

- A) Thursday, November 14 (10 calendar days)
- B) Thursday, November 14 or next working day
- C) Monday, November 18 (10 working days)
- D) Friday, November 15 (10 working days if holidays excluded)

<details>
<summary>Show Answer</summary>

**C) Monday, November 18 (10 working days)**

Legea 544/2001 specifies 10 working days (zile lucrătoare), not calendar days. Count only Monday-Friday, excluding legal holidays. Starting from November 4 (Monday), the 10th working day is November 18.
</details>

### Question 3
A company requests detailed pricing from competing bids in a completed procurement. They claim "commercial secret." What should you do?

- A) Accept the commercial secret claim and refuse
- B) Provide only the winning bid details
- C) Provide all bid prices—after award, this is public
- D) Request the court to decide

<details>
<summary>Show Answer</summary>

**C) Provide all bid prices—after award, this is public**

After a procurement procedure is completed, submitted prices are no longer commercial secrets. The competitive process is over, and transparency in public spending requires disclosure. Prices submitted in public procurement are public information after the award decision.
</details>

### Question 4
Your institution received 200 FOIA requests last year. The annual report shows 15 were denied (7.5%) and 8 of those denials were appealed, with 6 appeals upheld. What does this indicate?

- A) Good performance—denial rate is low
- B) Poor performance—exemptions are being misapplied
- C) Excellent transparency—few people need to request information
- D) Insufficient data to assess

<details>
<summary>Show Answer</summary>

**B) Poor performance—exemptions are being misapplied**

While the denial rate (7.5%) is relatively low, the high appeal success rate (6/8 = 75%) indicates that exemptions are being incorrectly applied in most cases where they're challenged. This suggests a need for better training on exemption criteria and the public interest test.
</details>

## Key Takeaways

1. **Proactive transparency reduces workload** - Publish before asked; it reduces FOIA requests

2. **10 working days is the standard** - Mark your calendar; late responses invite complaints

3. **Exemptions are narrow, not broad** - When in doubt, disclose; courts favor transparency

4. **Anonymization beats exemption** - Redact personal data rather than withholding entire documents

5. **Track and report your statistics** - Annual FOIA reports are mandatory and reveal improvement areas
