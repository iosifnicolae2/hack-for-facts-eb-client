# Your First Lead

## Concept Introduction

Theory is important, but investigation skills are built through practice. In this lesson, you'll apply everything you've learned â€” follow the money, ethical considerations, the investigation funnel â€” to find your first real lead.

A lead isn't a story. It's a starting point worth pursuing. Your goal is to find something unusual, document what you see, and formulate the questions you'd need to answer to move it down the investigation funnel.

> **Key Insight:** The best way to learn investigative journalism is to investigate. Start small, document carefully, and build your skills with each lead you pursue.

## Core Explanation

### What Makes a Good Lead?

Not every anomaly is worth investigating. A good lead has:

| Characteristic | Why It Matters |
|----------------|----------------|
| **Significant gap** | Small variances might be rounding; large gaps (>30%) suggest real issues |
| **Public interest** | Involves taxpayer money, public services, or accountability |
| **Verifiable** | You can realistically obtain documents and sources |
| **Proportionate** | The story would be worth the investigation effort |

### Types of Leads in Budget Data

1. **Execution Rate Anomalies**
   - Very low execution (below 50%): Why wasn't money spent?
   - Very high execution (>100%): Where did extra money come from?

2. **Year-over-Year Changes**
   - Sudden drops: Did a priority disappear?
   - Sudden spikes: Emergency or opportunism?

3. **Category Comparisons**
   - Peer institutions with vastly different spending
   - Costs per unit (per student, per patient) that vary widely

4. **Timing Patterns**
   - Year-end spending surges
   - Pre-election spending increases

### Moving from Lead to Investigation

Once you have a lead, the next steps are:

1. **Expand the data** â€” Is this pattern isolated or widespread?
2. **Request documents** â€” Use Law 544/2001 to request procurement files, meeting minutes
3. **Identify sources** â€” Who would know why this happened?
4. **Apply ethics check** â€” Is this worth investigating proportionately?
