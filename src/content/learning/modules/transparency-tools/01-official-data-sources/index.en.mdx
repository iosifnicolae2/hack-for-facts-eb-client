# Official Data Sources

## Concept Introduction

Budget transparency in Romania has improved dramatically over the past decade. The data exists — you just need to know where to find it. But with multiple government portals, each serving different purposes, navigating the landscape can be confusing.

The good news? There are only **four main sources** you need to know. Each has a specific strength, and together they give you a complete picture of public finances.

> **Key Insight:** Romania publishes more budget data than most citizens realize. Your advantage is knowing where to look.

## Core Explanation

### The Four Pillars of Budget Transparency

Each source serves a distinct purpose in the budget transparency ecosystem:

| Source | Purpose | Use When |
|--------|---------|----------|
| **Ministry of Finance** | Policy & planning | You need budget laws, fiscal strategy, or national aggregates |
| **Forexebug** | Execution monitoring | You want real-time data on actual spending vs. plans |
| **SEAP** | Procurement tracking | You're following specific contracts or suppliers |
| **Court of Accounts** | Audit findings | You're looking for verified problems and irregularities |

### Pro Tips

**Start with Forexebug** when investigating a specific institution. It has the most granular, up-to-date execution data.

**Cross-reference with Court of Accounts** if you find anomalies. They may have already investigated and documented the issue.

**SEAP is your friend for procurement stories.** Every public contract above the threshold must be published here.

> Four official sources cover Romanian budget data: Ministry of Finance (laws and strategy), Forexebug (real-time execution), SEAP (procurement contracts), and Court of Accounts (audit findings). Bookmark all four — you'll need different ones for different questions.
