## Core Explanation: Romania's Spending Priorities

Here's where Romania's money actually goes:

| Rank | Category | % of Total | What it Covers |
|------|----------|------------|----------------|
| 1 | Social Protection | ~35% | Pensions, unemployment, welfare |
| 2 | Health | ~18% | Hospitals, doctors, medicines |
| 3 | Education | ~12% | Schools, universities, teachers |
| 4 | Economic Affairs | ~10% | Agriculture, transport, industry |
| 5 | Public Order | ~8% | Police, courts, prisons |
| 6 | Defense | ~6% | Military, NATO commitments |
| 7 | Other | ~11% | Housing, environment, culture |

### The Social Protection Giant

**Why is Social Protection so large?** One word: pensions.

Romania has an aging population and a pay-as-you-go pension system. Current workers' contributions pay for current retirees. With fewer workers and more retirees, this category keeps growing.

- ~9 million pensioners
- Average pension: ~2,000 RON/month
- This single expense is larger than Health + Education combined

> **Reality Check:** Many Romanians think too much goes to "bureaucrats" or "politicians." In reality, Social Protection (mostly pensions) dominates the budget. The actual administration costs are a small fraction.
