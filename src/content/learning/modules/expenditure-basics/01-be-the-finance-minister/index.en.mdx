# Be the Finance Minister

## Concept Introduction

Now that you understand where money comes from (taxes, contributions, EU funds), let's see where it goes. Romania spends about **43.5% of GDP** through public budgets â€” that's over **727 billion RON** per year (2024).

But here's the thing: most of us have opinions about government spending without knowing the actual numbers. We think "too much goes to X" or "not enough to Y" â€” but how does our intuition compare to reality?

> **Key Insight:** The question isn't just HOW MUCH is spent, but also: Is it spent where you think it should be? And does actual spending match your priorities?

## Core Explanation: Romania's Spending Priorities (COFOG 2024)

Here's where Romania's money actually goes:

| Rank | Category | % of Total | What it Covers |
|------|----------|------------|----------------|
| 1 | Social Protection | ~29-30% | Pensions (4.7M recipients), child allowances, unemployment, social assistance |
| 2 | Economic Affairs | ~16-17% | Transport (A7, railways), energy, agriculture, industry support |
| 3 | General Public Services | ~12-14% | Administration, **2.1% GDP for interest payments**, EU contributions |
| 4 | Health | ~10-11% | Hospitals (7.28 beds/1,000), doctors, medicines |
| 5 | Education | ~8-9% | Schools, universities, teacher salaries |
| 6 | Public Order | ~6-7% | Police, courts, prisons (116% capacity) |
| 7 | Defense | ~5% | Military, NATO commitments (2.26% GDP achieved) |
| 8 | Other | ~9-10% | Environment (1.9% GDP), housing, culture, recreation |

### The Social Protection Giant

**Why is Social Protection so large?** One word: pensions.

Romania has an aging population and a pay-as-you-go pension system. Current workers' contributions pay for current retirees. With only **1.27 workers per pensioner** (8 pensioners for every 10 employees), this category keeps growing.

- **4,703,349 pensioners** (December 2024)
- Average pension: **2,748 RON/month** (post-September 2024 reform)
- 82.56% received increases averaging **+622 RON/month** (+27%)
- This single expense is larger than Health + Education combined
- Regional disparity: BucureÈ™ti has 4 pensioners per 10 workers, while Teleorman has **14-15 pensioners per 10 workers**

> **Reality Check:** Many Romanians think too much goes to "bureaucrats" or "politicians." In reality, Social Protection (mostly pensions) dominates the budget. Interest payments on debt (2.1% of GDP) are also substantial â€” a consequence of Romania's **9.3% deficit** (the worst in the EU).
