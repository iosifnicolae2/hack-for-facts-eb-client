# The Execution Gap

## Concept Introduction

Governments love to announce big budgets: "We're allocating 10 billion for education!" But here's the question they hope you don't ask: **How much of that money was actually spent?**

The gap between planned budget and actual spending is measured by the **execution rate**. This simple metric reveals more about real priorities than any press release ever could.

> **Key Insight:** Always check execution rates, not just allocations. A ministry can claim a "record budget" while actually spending less than the year before. The execution rate tells the truth.

## Core Explanation: Why the Gap Matters

### The Execution Rate Formula

```
Execution Rate = (Actually Spent Ã· Planned Budget) Ã— 100%
```

**Example:**
- Planned: 100 billion RON for Education
- Spent: 85 billion RON
- Execution Rate: 85%

### What Different Rates Mean

| Rate | Status | What it Signals |
|------|--------|-----------------|
| >95% | ğŸŸ¢ Excellent | Money spent as planned |
| 80-94% | ğŸŸ¡ Good | Minor underspending, possibly savings |
| 60-79% | ğŸŸ  Concerning | Significant gap â€” investigate why |
| Below 60% | ğŸ”´ Poor | Major failure â€” promises not kept |

### The Pattern That Repeats Every Year

One pattern is remarkably consistent in Romania:

| Spending Type | Typical Execution | Why? |
|---------------|-------------------|------|
| Personnel (salaries) | ~95-100% | Must be paid â€” people expect their wages |
| Goods & Services | ~85-95% | Contracts signed, services rendered |
| Transfers & Other | ~80-90% | Social assistance, grants â€” mostly automatic |
| **Investments** | **50-70%** | Projects delayed, procurement stuck |

> **ğŸ”´ The Investment Problem:** This is where Romania's development gets stuck. Money is budgeted for schools, hospitals, and roads â€” but year after year, 30-50% of it goes unspent. The new hospital wing? Delayed. The road repair? Next year. The school renovation? Still waiting.

### Why Do Investments Execute So Poorly?

1. **Complex procurement** â€” Public tender rules take 6-12 months
2. **Weak project preparation** â€” Projects aren't "shovel-ready"
3. **Capacity constraints** â€” Not enough people to manage projects
4. **Political interference** â€” Priorities shift with elections
5. **EU fund conditions** â€” Complex requirements cause delays
