# Local Government Structure

## Concept Introduction

Romania is divided into approximately **3,229 administrative-territorial units (UATs)**. These range from Bucharest with 2+ million people to tiny communes with just a few hundred residents. Each has its own budget, its own mayor, and its own local council.

The key challenge? Most local governments don't generate enough revenue locally â€” they depend heavily on transfers from the central government. This creates a power dynamic that affects how your local services are funded.

> **Key Insight:** About 85% of local government money comes from the center. This means local governments have limited fiscal autonomy â€” they can't fully control their own revenue.

### Revenue Sources Breakdown

| Source | Percentage | Description |
|--------|------------|-------------|
| **Income Tax Share** | ~45% | Portion of national income tax redistributed to localities |
| **Equalization Grants** | ~30% | Funds to help poorer areas match richer ones |
| **Special Grants** | ~10% | Earmarked for specific projects (EU co-financing, etc.) |
| **Own Revenues** | ~15% | Property tax, local fees, fines, service charges |
