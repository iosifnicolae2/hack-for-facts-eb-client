# Local Government Structure

## Concept Introduction

Romania is divided into **3,228 administrative-territorial units (UATs)** at the base level. These range from Bucharest with 2+ million people to tiny communes with just a few hundred residents. Each has its own budget, its own mayor, and its own local council.

The key challenge? Most local governments don't generate enough revenue locally â€” they depend heavily on transfers from the central government. This creates a power dynamic that affects how your local services are funded.

> **Key Insight:** About 85% of local government money comes from the center. This means local governments have limited fiscal autonomy â€” they can't fully control their own revenue.

### Revenue Sources Breakdown

| Source | Percentage | Description |
|--------|------------|-------------|
| **Income Tax Share** | ~25-32% | Portion of national income tax redistributed to localities (82% of income tax is redistributed: 47% to municipalities/towns/communes, 13% to county councils, 22% to equalization) |
| **Equalization Grants** | ~10-17% | Funds allocated through a formula: 70% based on fiscal capacity, 30% based on land area |
| **Earmarked Grants** | ~28-38% | Largest category â€” includes education funding (teacher salaries), social programs, fuel subsidies, capital projects |
| **Own Revenues** | ~14-15% | Property tax, local fees, fines, service charges |

> **Note:** Earmarked grants are the largest transfer category. Education funding alone accounts for ~28% of local revenue â€” local governments act as "paymasters" for centrally-determined teacher salaries without policy control.
