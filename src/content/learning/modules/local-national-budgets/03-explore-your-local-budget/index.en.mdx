# Explore Your Local Budget

## Concept Introduction

Now that you understand how local governments are structured and funded, it's time to find YOUR local budget. Whether you live in a major city or a small village, your UAT has budget data available on Transparenta.eu.

Local budgets are the best place to start your budget monitoring journey. They're smaller, more understandable, and the decision-makers â€” your mayor and local council â€” are accessible. You can actually attend council meetings and ask questions.

> **Key Insight:** Start local, build skills, then scale up. Local budgets are easier to monitor, understand, and influence than national ones.

## Guided Exercise: Explore Your Local Budget

Follow these steps to conduct your first local budget analysis:

### Step 1: Find Your UAT

1. Go to the [Entity Analytics](/entity-analytics) page on Transparenta.eu
2. Type your city, town, or commune name in the search box
3. Select your UAT from the dropdown results

<ExpandableHint trigger="Not sure which UAT is yours?">

**Villages are part of communes.** If you live in a village, search for the commune name instead. For example, if you live in the village of FloreÈ™ti near Cluj, search for "Comuna FloreÈ™ti" rather than just "FloreÈ™ti."

**Cities may have different official names.** Try variations like "Municipiul Cluj-Napoca" vs "Cluj-Napoca" or "OraÈ™ul Turda" vs "Turda."

</ExpandableHint>

### Step 2: Note Key Metrics

Once you've found your UAT, write down these numbers:

| Metric | Your UAT's Value | What to Look For |
|--------|------------------|------------------|
| **Total Budget** | _____________ RON | Is it growing or shrinking year-over-year? |
| **Per Capita** | _____________ RON | Compare this to neighboring UATs |
| **Execution Rate** | _____________% | Below 80% = worth investigating |
| **Top Spending Category** | _____________ | What's your UAT's biggest priority? |

### Step 3: Compare with Neighbors

Now find 2-3 similar localities to compare:

1. Click "Compare with Similar" or navigate to the [Map](/map) view
2. Choose UATs of similar size in your county
3. Compare per-capita spending (not totals â€” size matters!)

<ExpandableHint trigger="What counts as 'similar'?">

**Good comparisons:**
- Same UAT type (municipality vs. municipality, commune vs. commune)
- Similar population (within 50% of each other)
- Same county or neighboring counties
- Similar economic profile (urban vs. rural)

**Watch out for:**
- Comparing a county seat to a small commune
- Comparing a tourist town to an agricultural commune
- Comparing across very different regions

</ExpandableHint>

Based on what you found, identify one question worth investigating:

**Template questions:**
- "Why is our per-capita spending [higher/lower] than [comparison UAT]?"
- "Why is our investment execution rate only [X]%?"
- "Why does [category] consume [X]% of our budget?"
