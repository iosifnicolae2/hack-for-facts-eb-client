# Economic Classification

## Concept Introduction

Economic classification answers: **"What TYPE of expense is this?"** While functional classification tells you the purpose (education, health), economic classification tells you the nature of the spending â€” is it salaries, utilities, or construction?

This matters enormously because it reveals whether money goes to **operations** (keeping things running) or **investments** (building for the future).

> **Key Insight:** Code 71 (Capital Investments) is the most watched code in budget analysis. It consistently has the lowest execution rates, meaning promised infrastructure often doesn't get built.

## Core Explanation

### The Big Picture: Two Types of Spending

| Type | Key Codes | Nature | Budget Behavior |
|------|-----------|--------|-----------------|
| **Current Expenses** | 10, 20, 30, 40, 51, 55, 57 | Day-to-day operations | High execution (85%+) |
| **Capital Expenses** | 71, 72 | Long-term investments | Low execution (50-70%) |

**Current expense codes explained:**
- **10** = Personnel, **20** = Goods & services, **30** = Interest (on debt)
- **40** = Subsidies, **51** = Inter-government transfers, **55** = Other transfers
- **57** = Social assistance (major category at local level)

### Most Important Economic Codes

**Code 10 â€” Personnel Costs** (Cheltuieli de personal)
- **10.01**: Cash salaries (wages, bonuses, overtime) â€” bonuses are at 10.01.04-10.01.06
- **10.02**: In-kind salaries (holiday vouchers, meal tickets)
- **10.03**: Employer contributions (social security, health)
- Treated as obligatory fixed costs â€” typically near-full execution
- If significantly under budget, may indicate unfilled positions

**Code 20 â€” Goods and Services** (Bunuri È™i servicii)
- **20.01**: Operating goods (includes utilities at 20.01.03-04)
- **20.02**: Current repairs (maintenance)
- **20.05**: Inventory items (equipment, linens, tools)
- **20.30**: Other expenses â€” often contains consulting contracts
- Has intermediate flexibility in budget rectifications

**Code 71 â€” Capital Investments** âš ï¸ (Active nefinanciare)
- **71.01**: Fixed assets (buildings, equipment, vehicles)
- **71.03**: Capital repairs (large-scale rehabilitation)
- **THE RED FLAG CODE**: verified execution of only 53.7% by October 2022
- Of 140 priority investment projects tracked by the Ministry, 68 had 0% physical execution
- When this underperforms, promised infrastructure isn't being delivered
