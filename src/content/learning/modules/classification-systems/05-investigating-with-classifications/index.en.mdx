## You Found a Red Flag. Now What?

Spotting a budget problem is just the beginning. The real skill is **investigating** what's behind it.

When the Red Flags module teaches you to look for low investment execution or unusual spending patterns, your classification knowledge becomes the investigative tool. Without it, you know *something* is wrong â€” with it, you know *what specifically* failed and *where to dig deeper*.

> **Key Insight:** Classifications are your microscope. Red flags tell you where to look; classifications show you what you're seeing.

### Investigation Example 1: Low Investment Execution

**Red Flag:** A city has only 45% execution on capital investments (Code 71).

**Without classification knowledge:** "They didn't spend investment money." *(vague, no direction)*

**With classification knowledge:**

**Step 1 â€” Drill into Economic Sub-codes:**
| Sub-code | Name | Execution | Signal |
|----------|------|-----------|--------|
| 71.01.01 | Construction | 38% | Major issue |
| 71.01.02 | Equipment | 62% | Moderate |
| 71.01.03 | Vehicles | 95% | Fine |
| 71.03 | Major Repairs | 28% | Critical |

**Step 2 â€” Drill into Functional Classifications:**
| Function | Code 71 Execution | Meaning |
|----------|-------------------|---------|
| Education (65.02) | 32% | School construction stalled |
| Health (66.02) | 41% | Hospital equipment delayed |
| Housing (70.02) | 55% | Some housing projects progressed |

**Step 3 â€” Formulate Targeted Questions:**
- "Why did school construction (65.02 + 71.01.01) achieve only 32% execution?"
- "Were procurement procedures blocked? Land issues? Contractor problems?"
- "What specific schools were planned but not built?"

**Result:** Instead of asking "why wasn't money spent?", you can ask "why wasn't the new middle school on Strada Victoriei built when 4.2 million RON was allocated?"

## Investigation Example 2: Personnel Crowding Out Investment

**Red Flag:** Personnel costs are 68% of total budget (above the 60% warning threshold).

**Step 1 â€” Identify which functions are affected:**

| Function | Personnel % | Investment % | Pattern |
|----------|-------------|--------------|---------|
| Education (65.02) | 72% | 8% | Severe crowding |
| Health (66.02) | 64% | 12% | Moderate |
| Culture (67.02) | 45% | 35% | Balanced |
| Public Order (61.02) | 55% | 25% | Balanced |

**Step 2 â€” Focus on the worst case (Education):**
- Code 10 (personnel) at 72% means almost all money goes to salaries
- Code 71 (investments) at 8% means virtually no infrastructure improvements
- **What's suffering?** School renovations, new equipment, building repairs

**Step 3 â€” Formulate Investigation Questions:**
- "How many teaching positions were added in the last 3 years versus infrastructure investments?"
- "Which schools are in the worst physical condition, and are they receiving any capital funding?"
- "Is the high personnel percentage due to overstaffing or underfunding of total budget?"

## Cross-Referencing: The Power Move

The most powerful investigation technique combines **multiple classification perspectives**:

<ExpandableHint trigger="Investigation Scenario: The Education Budget Mystery">

**The Red Flags:**
1. Education (65.02) has 95% personnel execution but only 35% investment execution
2. Total education spending is growing 15% per year
3. Parent complaints about deteriorating school buildings

**Cross-Reference Investigation:**

| Question | Classification Path | What You Find |
|----------|---------------------|---------------|
| Where's the money going? | 65.02 â†’ Code 10 | Salaries growing fast |
| What's NOT happening? | 65.02 â†’ Code 71.01 | No new construction |
| What about repairs? | 65.02 â†’ Code 71.03 | Major repairs at 28% |
| Which schools? | 65.02.04 vs 65.02.03 | Secondary worse than primary |

**Root Cause Hypothesis:** New teaching hires are absorbing budget growth, leaving nothing for building maintenance. Secondary schools (65.02.04) are hit hardest, possibly due to enrollment-based staffing formulas.

**FOIA Question:** "Please provide the breakdown of personnel growth (number of positions and salary increases) versus capital expenditure for code 65.02 over the past 5 years."

</ExpandableHint>
