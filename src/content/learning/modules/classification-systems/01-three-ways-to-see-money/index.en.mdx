# Three Ways to See Money

## Concept Introduction

Budget documents look intimidating because they're full of numbers and codes. But once you crack the system, they become readable â€” and powerful.

In the Expenditure module, you learned about two ways to view spending: **Functional** (what for?) and **Economic** (what type?). Every single expense in the public budget can be viewed through **three lenses**. Think of it like describing a person: you could say what department they work for (organizational), what their job is about (functional), or how they're paid (economic).

> **Key Insight:** Every budget line answers three questions: WHO spends it, WHAT is it FOR, and WHAT TYPE of expense is it.

## Core Explanation

### Why Three Systems?

Each classification system serves a different purpose:

| System | Question | Purpose | Example Code |
|--------|----------|---------|--------------|
| **Organizational** | WHO spends? | Accountability â€” trace money to responsible entity | Ministry of Health |
| **Functional** | WHAT FOR? | Purpose â€” understand policy priorities | 66 (Health)* |
| **Economic** | WHAT TYPE? | Nature â€” distinguish salaries from investments | 10 (Personnel) |

> *Note: Romania uses its own national classification codes (established by Order MFP 1954/2005), which differ from the international COFOG standard. For example, Romanian code 66 = Health maps to COFOG 07, and Romanian code 65 = Education maps to COFOG 09.

### How They Work Together

When you see a budget line like `65.02.04.01 - 71.01.01`, you're seeing:

- **65.02.04.01** = Functional classification (Education â†’ Local level â†’ Secondary â†’ Lower cycle)
- **71.01.01** = Economic classification (Capital investments â†’ Fixed assets â†’ Construction)

Together they tell you: "Construction for a middle school, funded from local budget."

> **Map Analogy:** Imagine describing a trip. You can say WHERE you're going (functional), HOW you're getting there (economic), or WHO is arranging it (organizational). All three describe the same trip from different angles.
