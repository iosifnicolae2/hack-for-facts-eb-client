## Core Explanation

### Understanding Your Institution's Profile

When citizens, journalists, or oversight bodies look at your institution on Transparenta.eu, they see several key metrics:

| Metric | What It Shows | Questions It May Raise |
|--------|---------------|------------------------|
| **Execution Rate** | % of budget actually spent | Low: Why wasn't money spent? High (>100%): Where did extra come from? |
| **Personnel Share** | % of budget on salaries | High: Is staffing appropriate? Low: Are services understaffed? |
| **Capital Execution** | Investment completion | Low: Are projects delayed? What's blocking infrastructure? |
| **Year-over-Year Change** | Spending trends | Sudden changes: What's driving this? |

### Why Peer Comparison Matters

Looking at your institution in isolation tells you little. Comparing with similar institutions reveals:

- **Performance gaps:** Where are you behind peers?
- **Best practices:** What are high-performers doing differently?
- **Context:** Is low execution a local problem or systemic?
- **Questions to anticipate:** What will critics ask about?

### Preparing for Scrutiny

When a journalist or citizen examines your institution's data, they typically ask:

1. **Why is execution low/high?** â€” Have an explanation ready
2. **Why the high personnel costs?** â€” Explain staffing needs and controls
3. **Why are capital projects delayed?** â€” Document barriers (permits, procurement, weather)
4. **How do you compare to peers?** â€” Know your ranking and explain differences

Proactive preparation prevents defensive crisis management.
