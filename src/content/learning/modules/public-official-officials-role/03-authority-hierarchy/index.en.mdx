## Core Explanation

### The Three Tiers Explained

#### Ordonator Principal de Credite (OPC) â€” Primary Level

**Who:** Ministers, Mayors (Primari), County Council Presidents (PreÈ™edinÈ›i CJ)

**Authority:**
- Approve and modify the institution's overall budget
- Authorize credit transfers between budget chapters
- Full commitment and payment authority within legal limits
- Delegate authority to OSC and OTC levels

**Reports to:** Parliament (for state budget), Local/County Council (for local budgets)

#### Ordonator Secundar de Credite (OSC) â€” Secondary Level

**Who:** County School Inspectorates (ISJ), Public Health Directorates (DSP), Prefectures, deconcentrated services

**Authority:**
- Distribute credits received from OPC to subordinate OTCs
- Coordinate and monitor OTC budget execution
- Limited credit transfer authority (within approved limits)
- Submit consolidated reports to OPC

**Reports to:** Respective OPC (Ministry, Mayor, County Council President)

#### Ordonator TerÈ›iar de Credite (OTC) â€” Tertiary Level

**Who:** Individual schools, hospitals, cultural institutions, local agencies

**Authority:**
- Execute budget within approved allocations
- Authorize payments up to approved limits
- Cannot transfer credits between chapters
- Prepare and submit execution reports to OSC

**Reports to:** Respective OSC

### Delegation and Its Limits

An OPC can delegate authority to OSC and OTC levels, but:

- Delegation must be in writing
- Delegation doesn't transfer liability â€” both delegator and delegate can be held accountable
- Certain decisions cannot be delegated (e.g., approval of the annual budget)
- Delegation can be revoked at any time
