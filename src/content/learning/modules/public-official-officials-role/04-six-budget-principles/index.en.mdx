# Six Budget Principles

## Concept Introduction

Romania's public finance system is built on six foundational principles, legally mandated by Legea 500/2002 (the Public Finance Law). These aren't abstract ideals â€” they're legal requirements that govern every budget decision you make.

Violating these principles isn't just poor practice; it can trigger personal liability. Understanding them is essential for both compliance and excellence in public administration.

> **Key Insight:** The six principles aren't optional guidelines â€” they're legally mandated requirements. Every budget decision should be tested against them.

## Core Explanation

### The Six Principles in Practice

#### 1. Publicitate (Publicity) â€” Art. 9 ğŸ“¢

**Requirement:** Proactively disclose budget information to the public.

**Practical implication:** Budget approval decisions, execution reports, and major contracts must be published. Citizens have the right to request any budget information under Law 544/2001.

**Violation example:** Refusing to publish budget execution reports or claiming they're "internal documents."

#### 2. Unitate (Unity) â€” Art. 10 ğŸ“¦

**Requirement:** All revenues and expenses must be recorded in a single budget.

**Practical implication:** You cannot maintain separate "funds" or "accounts" outside the official budget. All money flows through one consolidated document.

**Violation example:** Keeping certain revenues (like donations or sponsorships) in a separate, unreported account.

#### 3. Universalitate (Universality) â€” Art. 8 â•

**Requirement:** Record all transactions at their gross (full) amounts.

**Practical implication:** If you receive 100,000 RON and spend 80,000 on collection costs, you record 100,000 revenue AND 80,000 expense â€” not just 20,000 net.

**Violation example:** Recording only net amounts or "netting" revenues against related expenses.

#### 4. Anualitate (Annuality) â€” Art. 11 ğŸ“…

**Requirement:** The budget is approved for one fiscal year (January 1 - December 31).

**Practical implication:** Commitments must be made within the budget year. Multi-year projects require special procedures. Funds don't automatically carry over.

**Violation example:** Committing funds in December for work that won't happen until the next year without proper multi-year authorization.

#### 5. Specializare (Specificity) â€” Art. 12 ğŸ¯

**Requirement:** Money can only be spent for the purposes specified in the budget classification.

**Practical implication:** If funds are allocated for "training" (economic code 20.13), you cannot use them for "office supplies" (20.01). Classification codes are legally binding.

**Violation example:** Using personnel budget (Chapter 10) to cover goods and services (Chapter 20) without proper transfer approval.

#### 6. Legalitate (Legality) â€” Art. 14(2) âš–ï¸

**Requirement:** Every payment must have a legal basis â€” a law, contract, or regulation that authorizes it.

**Practical implication:** Before approving any payment, you must be able to point to the specific legal provision that allows it. No legal basis = no payment.

**Violation example:** Paying bonuses without a regulation or collective agreement that authorizes them.
