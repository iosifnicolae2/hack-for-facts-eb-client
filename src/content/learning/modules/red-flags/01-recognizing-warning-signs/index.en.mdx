# Recognizing Budget Warning Signs

## Concept Introduction

Budget problems leave traces. Once you know what to look for, patterns emerge. A government that's struggling financially, mismanaging resources, or potentially engaging in corruption will show telltale signs in its budget data.

The key insight that separates casual observers from effective watchdogs is understanding that **context matters**. A single anomaly might have an innocent explanation â€” a construction project delayed by weather, or an unexpected emergency expense. But when multiple warning signs appear together, that's when you should pay attention.

> **Key Insight:** A single red flag isn't proof of wrongdoing â€” but multiple red flags together warrant investigation.

## Understanding Red Flag Severity

Not all warning signs are equally serious. Understanding severity helps you prioritize your investigations:

| Severity | Meaning | Action |

### The Compound Effect

One red flag might be noise. Three red flags together form a pattern. When you see:

- **2-3 flags**: Worth monitoring
- **4-5 flags**: Warrants active investigation
- **6+ flags**: Serious concern â€” document everything
