# Applying Red Flag Analysis

## Concept Introduction

You've learned to recognize the seven major red flags. Now it's time to apply that knowledge. In this lesson, you'll analyze a case study, understand Romania's broader fiscal challenges, and practice finding red flags in real data.

Understanding the macro context is essential because local budget problems often connect to national fiscal pressures. When the country runs a large deficit, the pressure flows down to local governments.

> **Key Insight:** Local red flags often reflect national fiscal stress. Understanding both levels makes you a more effective watchdog.

### What the Deficit Means for You

The deficit must be closed. There are only three ways:

| Option | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| **Higher Taxes** | VAT increase to 21% (effective Aug 2025), possible income tax changes |
| **Reduced Services** | Cuts to programs, delayed investments, service degradation |
| **Inflation** | "Stealth tax" that erodes savings and purchasing power |

**Why this matters for budget oversight:** When money is tight, every lei matters more. Waste, inefficiency, and corruption become even more damaging. This is why budget oversight is crucial right now.

## Guided Activity: Hunt for Red Flags

Now apply your skills to real data. Follow these steps to analyze your local government:

### Step 1: Open Entity Analytics

Navigate to the Entity Analytics page on Transparenta.eu to access detailed budget data.

[Open Entity Analytics â†’](/entities)

### Step 2: Find Your Local Government

Search for your city, town, or commune. Open the detailed view to see budget breakdowns.

### Step 3: Check for Each Red Flag

Use this checklist to systematically review:

- [ ] **Investment execution rate (Code 71)** â€” Is it below 70%?
- [ ] **Personnel as % of total budget** â€” Is it above 60%?
- [ ] **Number of rectifications** â€” Are there 3 or more?
- [ ] **Year-over-year spending change** â€” Is it above 20%?
- [ ] **Look for arrears** â€” Any unpaid bills reported?

### Step 4: Record Your Findings

Document what you find:
- How many potential red flags did you identify?
- What questions would you ask based on the data?
- Are there patterns that concern you?

### Step 5: Compare with Neighbors

Context matters. Compare your locality with similar neighbors:
- Do they have the same issues?
- Or is your locality an outlier?
- What explains the differences?
