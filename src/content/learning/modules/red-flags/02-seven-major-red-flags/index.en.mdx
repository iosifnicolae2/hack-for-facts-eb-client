## The Seven Red Flags in Detail

### ðŸš© Red Flag #1: Low Execution Rates

**What It Looks Like:**
- Investment (Code 71) execution below 70%
- Money allocated but not spent
- Especially common for capital projects

**Why It's a Problem:**
- Infrastructure promised but not delivered
- Schools, hospitals, roads not built
- EU funds at risk of being lost

**Questions to Ask:**
- "Why was only X% of the investment budget spent?"
- "Which projects were cancelled or delayed?"
- "Will the money be reallocated or lost?"

> **Example:** City X planned 50M RON for road repairs. Actual spending: 22M RON (44%). Result: Only 3 of 7 planned roads were repaired.

---

### ðŸš© Red Flag #2: Frequent Rectifications

**What It Looks Like:**
- 3+ budget amendments in a single year
- Large changes (>15% of original budget)
- Changes timed near elections

**Why It's a Problem:**
- Poor initial planning
- Political manipulation of priorities
- Unreliable budget as policy tool

**Questions to Ask:**
- "Why was the original budget so inaccurate?"
- "Who benefited from each rectification?"
- "Were any cuts made to fund increases elsewhere?"

---

### ðŸš© Red Flag #3: Arrears (Arierate)

**What It Looks Like:**
- Unpaid bills accumulating
- "Arrears" appearing in execution reports
- Growing debt to suppliers

**Why It's a Problem:**
- Institution is spending beyond its means
- Suppliers may stop providing services
- Hidden debt that must eventually be paid

**Questions to Ask:**
- "What is the current arrears balance?"
- "How long have these bills been unpaid?"
- "Is there a plan to clear the arrears?"

---

### ðŸš© Red Flag #4: Personnel Crowding Out Investment

**What It Looks Like:**
- Personnel costs >60% of total budget
- Investment consistently \<10%
- Salary execution 99%+, investment \<60%

**Why It's a Problem:**
- No money for development
- Institution maintaining status quo only
- Infrastructure deteriorating

**Questions to Ask:**
- "What is the personnel-to-investment ratio?"
- "Has staffing increased while investment decreased?"
- "What development projects are being sacrificed?"

---

- Avoids competitive bidding
- Often leads to higher prices
- May indicate favoritism or kickbacks

### ðŸš© Red Flag #6: Emergency Procurement Abuse

**What It Looks Like:**
- High percentage of "emergency" procedures
- Non-emergency items procured urgently
- Same suppliers winning repeatedly

**Why It's a Problem:**
- Bypasses competitive procedures
- Often results in higher prices
- May indicate collusion or corruption

**Questions to Ask:**
- "What was the emergency that justified this?"
- "Why wasn't this planned in advance?"
- "How does this price compare to market rates?"

---

### ðŸš© Red Flag #7: Opaque Transfers

**What It Looks Like:**
- Large amounts in "Other transfers" categories
- Money moving between entities without clear purpose
- Transfers to organizations with unclear mandates

**Why It's a Problem:**
- Hides true spending purposes
- May fund activities outside normal oversight
- Creates opportunities for misuse

**Questions to Ask:**
- "What specific activities do these transfers fund?"
- "Who are the ultimate recipients?"
- "What outcomes were achieved?"
