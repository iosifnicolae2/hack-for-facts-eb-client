## Introduction

> **Key Insight**: Most procurement violations occur during execution—not planning. The moment you publish a procedure, every action is subject to challenge and potential personal liability.

Once a procurement procedure is launched, strict rules apply to every step. Understanding the legal constraints prevents costly challenges, delays, and personal sanctions.

### Procedure Types Summary

| Procedure | When to Use | Min. Days | Key Features |
|-----------|-------------|-----------|--------------|
| **Direct Acquisition** | Below threshold | N/A | Simple, 1 quote OK |
| **Simplified** | Mid-range values | 6-10 | Fewer formalities |
| **Open Tender** | Above threshold, clear specs | 30-35 | Most common |
| **Restricted** | Need pre-qualification | 30+25 | Two-stage |
| **Competitive Negotiation** | Complex, need flexibility | 30 | Can negotiate |
| **Competitive Dialogue** | Cannot define specs | 30 | For innovation |
| **Negotiation w/o Notice** | Emergency, sole source | 0 | Exceptional only |

### Evaluation Best Practices

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Score exactly as per published methodology | Invent new criteria during evaluation |
| Document every decision with reasons | Leave scoring unexplained |
| Use SEAP for all communications | Call or email tenderers directly |
| Allow correction of minor formalities | Reject for minor administrative errors |
| Request clarification if unclear | Assume tenderer's intent |
| Check abnormally low prices | Accept unusually low prices without verification |

### Amendment Limits Summary

| Modification Type | Limit | Requires |
|-------------------|-------|----------|
| **De minimis** | 10% (goods/services) / 15% (works) AND under threshold | ANAP notification |
| **Additional works/services** | 50% cumulative | Justification, ANAP notification |
| **Unforeseen circumstances** | 50% cumulative | Documentation, ANAP notification |
| **New value exceeds threshold** | N/A | NEW PROCEDURE required |

## Knowledge Check

Test your understanding of procurement execution.

### Question 1
You receive a challenge (contestație) against your award decision on day 5 of the standstill period. What must you do?

- A) Ignore it and sign the contract on day 12
- B) Suspend the procedure and respond to CNSC within 5 working days
- C) Immediately cancel the procedure
- D) Sign quickly before the challenge is processed

<details>
<summary>Show Answer</summary>

**B) Suspend the procedure and respond to CNSC within 5 working days**

A valid challenge automatically suspends the procedure. You must compile your procurement file and respond to CNSC within 5 working days. Signing during suspension or ignoring the challenge can result in contract nullity and personal liability.
</details>

### Question 2
A works contract of 800,000 lei needs an additional 180,000 lei for unforeseen ground conditions. Can this be done through a modification?

- A) No, all modifications require a new procedure
- B) Yes, this is within the 50% cumulative limit
- C) No, the new total exceeds the works threshold
- D) Yes, but only if predicted in original documentation

<details>
<summary>Show Answer</summary>

**B) Yes, this is within the 50% cumulative limit**

The original value is 800,000 lei. The 50% limit = 400,000 lei. The proposed modification of 180,000 lei (22.5%) is within this limit. The new total of 980,000 lei exceeds the works threshold of 900,400 lei, but since this is a modification to an existing contract (not a new procurement), the threshold test applies only to determine whether a new procedure is needed—which it isn't since Art. 221 modification rules apply.
</details>

### Question 3
During evaluation, a committee member realizes that an important criterion was omitted from the documentation. What should they do?

- A) Add the criterion and evaluate all offers against it
- B) Cancel the procedure and re-launch with correct criteria
- C) Evaluate without the criterion as published
- D) Contact the preferred tenderer to check if they meet it

<details>
<summary>Show Answer</summary>

**C) Evaluate without the criterion as published**

Evaluation must be exactly as per the published documentation. Adding new criteria during evaluation is a serious violation. If the missing criterion is essential, the procedure might need cancellation, but the evaluation itself must follow published rules. The decision to cancel should be made by the contracting authority, not unilaterally by the committee.
</details>

### Question 4
The standstill period for a contract above EU thresholds ends on a Sunday. When can you sign?

- A) Sunday (the 11th day)
- B) Monday (first working day after)
- C) Friday before (last working day of the period)
- D) Tuesday (12th calendar day)

<details>
<summary>Show Answer</summary>

**B) Monday (first working day after)**

The standstill period is 11 calendar days, but if it ends on a non-working day, it extends to the next working day. You cannot sign on Sunday (non-working), and signing Friday would be before the period ends. Monday is the first permissible date.
</details>

## Key Takeaways

1. **Procedure selection affects everything** - Choose based on situation, not just value; wrong procedure = cancellation risk

2. **Evaluation must match documentation exactly** - No new criteria, no methodology changes, everything through SEAP

3. **Standstill is mandatory** - Never sign before period ends; early signing can void the contract

4. **Modifications are strictly limited** - 50% cumulative, document unforeseen circumstances, notify ANAP

5. **Challenges suspend procedures** - Respond professionally, document thoroughly, implement decisions
