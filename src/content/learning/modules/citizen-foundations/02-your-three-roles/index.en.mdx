# Taxpayer, Beneficiary, Watchdog

You're not just a taxpayer. In fact, your most powerful role costs you nothing‚Äîand most people don't even know they have it.

Every citizen has three distinct relationships with public budgets. Most only think about the first one. By the end of this lesson, you'll understand all three‚Äîand know exactly which law protects your right to use the most important one.

> **After this lesson, you'll be able to:** Describe your three budget-related roles and identify the Romanian law that protects your oversight rights.

## Role 1: Taxpayer üí∞

Every purchase you make includes VAT (21% standard rate since August 2025). Every paycheck has income tax and social contributions deducted.

For a 5,000 RON gross salary:
- **1,250 RON** ‚Üí Pension fund (CAS - 25%)
- **500 RON** ‚Üí Health insurance (CASS - 10%)
- **325 RON** ‚Üí Income tax (10% of taxable base)
- **Total: ~2,075 RON** (about 41.5% of gross)

This money funds everything from your local road repairs to national defense.

<Hidden trigger="Quick check: What are the three main ways you contribute as a taxpayer?">
1. **Income tax** (10% of taxable income)
2. **Social contributions** (CAS 25% + CASS 10%)
3. **VAT** (21% on most purchases)
</Hidden>

## Role 2: Beneficiary üìã

Don't just think about what you pay‚Äîthink about what you receive:

| Service | Annual Value per Person (approx.) |
|---------|-----------------------------------|
| Transport infrastructure | ~1,500 RON |
| Public healthcare | ~4,500 RON |

**Here's what surprises most people:** The average citizen receives more in services than they pay in taxes. That's how progressive taxation and public goods work‚Äîthose who earn more contribute more, and essential services reach everyone.

## Role 3: Watchdog üëÅÔ∏è

This might be your most important role‚Äîand it's free to use.

**Law 544/2001** (*Legea privind liberul acces la informa»õiile de interes public*) guarantees your right to request any public information from any public institution.

You can ask:
- How much was spent on a specific project?
- Who won a public contract and why?
- What's the salary structure in a public institution?
- Why was a budget line underspent?

Institutions must respond within 10 working days (or 30 days for complex requests).

**Transparenta.eu makes exercising this role easier** by pre-analyzing public budget data‚Äîso you can spot patterns first, then ask targeted questions.

## Key Takeaway

> You are simultaneously a **taxpayer** (funding the system with ~41.5% of your salary), a **beneficiary** (receiving public services), and a **watchdog** (with legal rights to oversight). The watchdog role‚Äîguaranteed by **Law 544/2001**‚Äîis what makes the other two roles accountable.

**Most citizens only know their taxpayer role.** Now you know all three. In the next lesson, you'll see exactly where your tax money flows‚Äîfrom your paycheck to public services.
