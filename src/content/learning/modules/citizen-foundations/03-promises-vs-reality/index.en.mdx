# Promises vs Reality

## Concept Introduction

Politicians make promises. Budgets are planned. But what actually happens? The gap between what was **planned** and what was **spent** reveals the true priorities of any government â€” not their words, but their actions.

This gap is measured by the **execution rate**: the percentage of planned budget that was actually spent. An execution rate of 100% means everything planned was spent. Below 70%? That's a significant gap worth investigating.

> **Key Insight:** The budget reveals what a society truly values â€” not what politicians say, but where they actually put the money.

## Core Explanation

### Understanding Execution Rates

| Execution Rate | Meaning | Signal |
|----------------|---------|--------|
| 95-100% | Budget fully utilized | Normal operation |
| 70-95% | Moderate underspending | Minor delays or savings |
| 50-70% | Significant gap | Planning issues or blocked projects |
| Below 50% | Major failure | Investigate: Why wasn't money spent? |
| Above 100% | Overspending | Budget revisions or emergency spending |

### The Deficit Paradox: How Can You Overspend AND Underspend?

Here's a puzzle: Romania had a **record deficit of 8.65% GDP in 2024** (152 billion RON vs. planned 86.6 billion RON). Yet domestic infrastructure investments only reached **55-60% execution**, while EU-financed projects performed even worse at **3-37%** depending on the program. How is this possible?

The answer reveals how public money actually flows:

| Category | What Happened | Effect |
|----------|---------------|--------|
| Personnel (salaries) | Grew 24%, **exceeded** initial plan | Significant overspend |
| Goods & Services | Grew 21%, **exceeded** initial plan | Significant overspend |
| Social Transfers | Rose 17%, **exceeded** plan | Significant overspend |
| Capital Investments | Only 55-60% spent | Major underspend |
| EU Projects | Only 3-37% absorbed* | Major underspend |

*\*EU absorption varies by program: PNRR 29-37%, Regional Programs 3.2%, Transport 27%*

**The pattern:** Overspending on recurring costs (salaries, goods, social benefits) was larger than underspending on investments. The money didn't disappear â€” it was **redirected** from building things to paying people.

### Why Does This Happen?

1. **Salaries are legally mandatory** â€” Public employees must be paid, and raises are hard to reverse
2. **Investments are easy to delay** â€” Construction can wait; salaries cannot
3. **Weak administrative capacity** â€” Institutions struggle to execute complex projects
4. **EU project complexity** â€” Strict conditions, audits, and procurement rules slow absorption
5. **Political incentives** â€” Visible salary increases win votes faster than multi-year infrastructure

### The Personnel vs Investment Pattern

One pattern repeats every year in Romania: **personnel costs execute at 98-100%+** while **capital investments execute at 50-62%**.

This means salaries always get paid (sometimes more than planned), but the new school, the hospital wing, the road repair? Often delayed to next year. And next year. And the year after.

The consequence: Romania risks losing **â‚¬5.2 billion** from EU structural funds due to absorption failures, and potentially **â‚¬10 billion** from PNRR (Recovery Fund) due to missed milestones.
