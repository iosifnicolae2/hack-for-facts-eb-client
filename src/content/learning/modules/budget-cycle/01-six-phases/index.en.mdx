# The Six Phases of the Budget Cycle

## Concept Introduction

The budget isn't a one-time decision â€” it's a continuous cycle that repeats every year. Understanding WHEN decisions are made helps you know WHEN to pay attention and engage effectively.

Think of it like farming seasons: there's a time to plant, a time to tend, and a time to harvest. Similarly, the budget has distinct phases where different activities happen. Miss the right window, and you wait another year.

> **Key Insight:** November-December is "budget season" â€” the most important time for citizen engagement. The Government must submit the budget to Parliament by **November 15** (not October 15 as older sources state), following amendments made by Law 270/2013.

## The Six Phases Explained

Every budget goes through these six phases. Here's what happens in each and when YOU can make a difference:

| Phase | Timing | What Happens | Legal Basis | Your Opportunity |
|-------|--------|--------------|-------------|------------------|
| **Planning** | Jun - Aug | Macroeconomic forecasts (Jun 1), spending ceilings (Jul 31), framework letter (Aug 1) | Art. 31-33 | Low - internal process |
| **Drafting** | Aug - Nov 15 | Institutions submit proposals (Sep 15), Ministry drafts budget (Sep 30), Government submits to Parliament (Nov 15) | Art. 34-35 | Medium - consultations may occur |
| **Approval** | Nov 15 - Dec | Parliament debates and votes; urgent procedure if not adopted by Dec 15 | Art. 17(2), 35(4) | **HIGH** - contact MPs, attend sessions |
| **Execution** | Jan - Dec | Institutions spend approved funds; rectifications possible (by Nov 30) | Art. 6, 61(1) | Medium - monitor execution rates |
| **Reporting** | Quarterly | Execution reports published; local budgets: Apr, Jul, Oct, Dec | Art. 44, 55; Law 273/2006 Art. 49(12) | Medium - analyze and question |
| **Audit** | Following year | Court of Accounts reviews spending, reports to Parliament by Jul 1 | Law 94/1992; Art. 56(4) | Low - read audit reports |

### Phase Details (Expanded Content)

**1. PLANNING (June-August)** â€” *Law 500/2002, Art. 31-33*
- **June 1:** Macroeconomic forecasts completed (Art. 31)
- **July 31:** Ministry submits spending ceilings to Government (Art. 32)
- **August 1:** Framework letter sent to principal credit authorizers (Art. 33)
- **Your opportunity:** Limited â€” this is an internal government process

**2. DRAFTING (August - November 15)** â€” *Law 500/2002, Art. 34-35*
- **September 15:** Institutions submit revised proposals to Ministry (Art. 34(5))
- **September 30:** Ministry delivers draft budget to Government for first reading (Art. 35(1))
- **November 15:** Government submits budget to Parliament (Art. 35(4)) â€” *amended from October 15 by Law 270/2013*
- **Your opportunity:** Public consultations may occur under Law 52/2003 (but Parliament is exempt)

**3. APPROVAL (November 15-December)** â€” *Law 500/2002, Art. 17(2), 35(4), 37*
- Parliament debates the budget in joint sittings
- Amendments proposed and voted
- **December 15:** If not adopted, urgent procedure required (Art. 17(2))
- If unapproved 3 days before year-end, previous year's provisions apply (Art. 37)
- **Your opportunity:** HIGH â€” contact your MPs, attend public sessions (note: no formal citizen input mechanism exists during Parliamentary consideration)

**4. EXECUTION (January-December)** â€” *Law 500/2002, Art. 6, 61(1)*
- Institutions spend according to approved budget
- Execution closes **December 31** (Art. 61(1))
- **Rectifications:** May occur, typically September-November; must be elaborated by **November 30** (Art. 6)
- Credit transfers between budget chapters allowed starting Q3
- **Your opportunity:** Monitor execution rates on Transparenta.eu

**5. REPORTING (Quarterly + Annual)** â€” *Law 500/2002, Art. 44, 55; Law 273/2006, Art. 49(12)*
- Principal credit authorizers prepare quarterly monitoring reports (Art. 44(1))
- Ministry publishes monthly execution notes
- **Local budgets:** Reports due in April (Q1), July (Q2), October (Q3), December (Q4) per Law 273/2006
- Annual execution account submitted to Parliament by **July 1** of following year (Art. 56(4))
- **Your opportunity:** Analyze data, identify gaps, ask questions

**6. AUDIT (Following Year)** â€” *Law 94/1992 (Court of Accounts Law)*
- **Curtea de Conturi** (Court of Accounts) reviews spending under Constitutional Article 140
- Reports prepared within 6 months of receiving accounts (Art. 29)
- Annual Public Report transmitted to Parliament
- Court also houses EU Funds Audit Authority under Law 77/2002
- **Your opportunity:** Read audit reports, follow up on findings with your MP

## Key Takeaway

> **Remember:** The budget cycle has six phases, but November-December is YOUR window. The legal deadline of **November 15** (not October 15 as older sources state) starts "budget season" â€” this is when your voice can influence how public money is allocated next year. For local budgets, you also have a 15-day contestation period under Law 273/2006.
