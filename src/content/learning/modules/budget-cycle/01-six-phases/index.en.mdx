# The Six Phases of the Budget Cycle

## Concept Introduction

The budget isn't a one-time decision â€” it's a continuous cycle that repeats every year. Understanding WHEN decisions are made helps you know WHEN to pay attention and engage effectively.

Think of it like farming seasons: there's a time to plant, a time to tend, and a time to harvest. Similarly, the budget has distinct phases where different activities happen. Miss the right window, and you wait another year.

> **Key Insight:** October-December is "budget season" â€” the most important time for citizen engagement. This is when Parliament debates and approves next year's spending.

## The Six Phases Explained

Every budget goes through these six phases. Here's what happens in each and when YOU can make a difference:

| Phase | Timing | What Happens | Your Opportunity |
|-------|--------|--------------|------------------|
| **Planning** | May - Aug | Ministry sets ceilings, institutions prepare requests | Low - internal process |
| **Drafting** | Sep - Oct 15 | Government consolidates requests | Medium - consultations may occur |
| **Approval** | Oct - Dec | Parliament debates and votes | **HIGH** - contact MPs, attend sessions |
| **Execution** | Jan - Dec | Institutions spend approved funds | Medium - monitor execution rates |
| **Reporting** | Quarterly | Execution reports published | Medium - analyze and question |
| **Audit** | Following year | Court of Accounts reviews spending | Low - read audit reports |

### Phase Details (Expanded Content)

**1. PLANNING (May-August)**
- Ministry of Finance sets spending ceilings
- Each institution prepares budget requests
- Internal negotiations happen
- **Your opportunity:** Limited â€” this is an internal government process

**2. DRAFTING (September - October 15)**
- Government consolidates all requests
- Budget draft prepared
- **Legal deadline: October 15** â€” must submit to Parliament
- **Your opportunity:** Public consultations may occur, watch for announcements

**3. APPROVAL (October-December)**
- Parliament debates the budget
- Amendments proposed and voted
- Final vote usually in December
- **Your opportunity:** HIGH â€” contact your MPs, attend public sessions, submit written opinions

**4. EXECUTION (January-December)**
- Institutions spend according to approved budget
- Quarterly reports published
- Rectifications may occur
- **Your opportunity:** Monitor execution rates on Transparenta.eu

**5. REPORTING (Quarterly + Annual)**
- Execution reports made public
- Comparison: planned vs. actual
- Available on Forexebug and ministry sites
- **Your opportunity:** Analyze data, identify gaps, ask questions

**6. AUDIT (Following Year)**
- Court of Accounts reviews spending
- Reports identify irregularities
- Recommendations issued
- **Your opportunity:** Read audit reports, follow up on findings
