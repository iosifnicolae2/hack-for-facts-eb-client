# Budget Rectifications Explained

## Concept Introduction

What happens when reality doesn't match the plan? Governments adjust their budgets mid-year through a process called **rectification**. Understanding rectifications helps you separate normal adjustments from red flags that indicate poor planning or broken promises.

Think of it like adjusting your personal budget: if you get a raise, you might allocate more to savings. If an unexpected expense comes up, you might cut entertainment spending. The government does the same thing â€” but with YOUR money.

> **Key Insight:** Some rectifications are normal and necessary. But frequent or large rectifications often signal poor planning, broken promises, or political manipulation.

---

## What Are Budget Rectifications?

A budget rectification is a **formal change to the approved annual budget** during the fiscal year. It requires a new law passed by Parliament, just like the original budget.

### Common Reasons for Rectifications

| Reason | Example | Concern Level |
|--------|---------|---------------|
| **Revenue changes** | Tax collection higher/lower than expected | Normal |
| **EU funds received** | New EU project approved | Normal |
| **Emergency spending** | Natural disaster response | Normal |
| **Political priorities** | Election-year spending increases | Watch carefully |
| **Poor initial planning** | Unrealistic revenue projections | Red flag |
| **Hidden spending** | Adding projects not in original budget | Red flag |

### Questions to Ask About Any Rectification

1. **Is it through OUG or Parliament?** OUG bypasses proper legislative debate
2. **What's the new deficit target?** Compare to original budget and EU limits (3% GDP)
3. **Is it an election year?** Pre-election spending spikes are common manipulation
4. **What changed from the original budget?** Large deviations suggest poor initial planning
5. **What does the Fiscal Council say?** Their opinions often highlight problems ignored by government

## Key Takeaway

> **Remember:** Rectifications reveal whether a government keeps its budget promises. Romania has been under EU Excessive Deficit Procedure since 2020, with deficits ranging from 6% to 9.3% of GDP. Watch for: OUG bypassing Parliament, Reserve Fund bypasses, pre-election spending spikes, and deficits far above targets. Use Transparenta.eu to compare original budgets with actual execution.
