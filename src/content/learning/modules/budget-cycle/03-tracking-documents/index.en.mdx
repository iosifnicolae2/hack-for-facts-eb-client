# Tracking Budget Documents

## Concept Introduction

Knowing WHAT to look for is just as important as knowing WHEN to look. Each phase of the budget cycle produces specific documents that tell different parts of the story. This lesson gives you a checklist of key documents and where to find them.

Think of it like tracking a court case: you need the original filing, the trial proceedings, and the verdict. For budgets, you need the proposal, the debates, and the execution reports.

> **Key Insight:** Budget transparency means documents are public â€” but they're scattered across multiple government websites. Knowing where to look saves hours of searching.

## Document Guide

### 1. During Approval (October-December)

| Document | Romanian Name | What It Contains | Where to Find |
|----------|---------------|------------------|---------------|
| **Budget Law Draft** | Proiectul legii bugetului de stat | Complete proposed budget for next year | cdep.ro, senat.ro |
| **Budget Annexes** | Anexe la buget (Anexa nr. 1, 2, etc.) | Detailed allocations by ministry/institution | mfinante.gov.ro |
| **Explanatory Memorandum** | Expunere de motive | Why amounts were chosen, economic projections | mfinante.gov.ro |

**Why these matter:** These documents show what the government PROMISES to spend. Compare them later to what actually happens.

### 2. During Execution (Monthly)

| Document | Romanian Name | What It Contains | Where to Find |
|----------|---------------|------------------|---------------|
| **Execution Reports** | Rapoarte de execuÈ›ie bugetarÄƒ | Actual spending vs. planned (published monthly) | mfinante.gov.ro, Forexebug |
| **Arrears Reports** | SituaÈ›ia arieratelor (Form F1118) | Unpaid debts over 90 days by government entities | mfinante.gov.ro |
| **Rectification Laws** | Legi de rectificare | Mid-year budget changes | cdep.ro |

**Why these matter:** These show reality vs. promises. Low execution rates mean money wasn't spent as planned. Monthly reporting allows early detection of problems.

### 3. After Audit (12-18 Months After Fiscal Year)

| Document | Romanian Name | What It Contains | Where to Find |
|----------|---------------|------------------|---------------|
| **Annual Public Report** | Raportul Public Anual | Comprehensive audit findings submitted to Parliament | curteadeconturi.ro |
| **Financial Audit Reports** | Rapoarte de Audit Financiar | Opinions on entity financial statements | curteadeconturi.ro |
| **Compliance Audit Reports** | Rapoarte de Audit de Conformitate | Regularity and legality verification | curteadeconturi.ro |
| **Performance Audit Reports** | Rapoarte de Audit al PerformanÈ›ei | Economy, efficiency, effectiveness evaluation | curteadeconturi.ro |
| **Follow-up Reports** | Rapoarte de Follow-up | Whether past problems were fixed | curteadeconturi.ro |

**Why these matter:** Independent verification of whether money was spent legally and effectively. Note: Reports typically publish 12-18 months after the fiscal year ends, not immediately in June.

## Quick Reference: Key Websites

| Site | URL | What You'll Find |
|------|-----|------------------|
| **Ministry of Finance** | mfinante.gov.ro | Budget drafts, execution reports, fiscal data |
| **Budget Transparency Portal** | mfinante.gov.ro/apps/transparenta-bugetara | Monthly monitoring of ~13,700 public institutions |
| **Chamber of Deputies** | cdep.ro | Budget law debates, voting records, amendments |
| **Senate** | senat.ro | Budget law debates, committee discussions |
| **Court of Accounts** | curteadeconturi.ro | Audit reports, irregularity findings |
| **Forexebug (Main Portal)** | forexe.mfinante.gov.ro | Real-time execution data for ~14,000 public entities |
| **Forexebug (Public)** | forexepublic.mfinante.gov.ro | Public transparency portal for Forexebug data |
| **Open Data Portal** | data.gov.ro | Central government datasets including budget info |
| **Transparenta.eu** | transparenta.eu | Searchable, visualized budget data |

## Calendar: Your Budget Year

Set these reminders to stay informed:

| Date | Action | Why |
|------|--------|-----|
| **October 1** | Check for budget draft | Budget season begins |
| **October 15** | Verify submission to Parliament | Legal deadline (Art. 35.4, Law 500/2002) |
| **November 15** | Review Parliament debates | Key discussion period |
| **December 15** | Target adoption date | Emergency procedures if not adopted |
| **Monthly** | Check execution reports | Monitor actual spending vs. planned |
| **12-18 months after fiscal year** | Read Court of Accounts reports | See audit findings (not June as commonly believed) |

## Key Takeaway

> **Remember:** Budget documents are public, but scattered across multiple websites. Bookmark key sources, set calendar reminders for budget season (October-December), and use Transparenta.eu alerts to get notified when new data is available for the institutions you care about.
